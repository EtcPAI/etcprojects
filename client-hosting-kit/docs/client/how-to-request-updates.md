# How to Request Updates to Your Website

Welcome! Here's how to get changes made to your site.

## The short version

Go to **https://updates.yourdomain.com**, pick your business, fill in the
form. We'll respond within one business day.

## What to include in a request

The more specific you are, the faster we can ship the change. Helpful details:

- **Which page or section?** e.g. "the homepage hero" or "the contact page".
- **What should change?** If it's text, paste the exact new wording. If it's
  an image, mention what you want and we'll follow up about getting the file.
- **How urgent?** The form has four options — pick honestly. "Whenever you
  get to it" is fine for most requests and helps us batch work efficiently.

## Examples of good requests

> **Page:** /about
> **Change:** Replace "Founded in 2020" with "Founded in 2019" in the second
> paragraph.

> **Page:** Homepage hero
> **Change:** Swap the headline from "Welcome" to "Trusted financial guidance
> since 2019". Keep the same subtitle.

> **Page:** Footer (all pages)
> **Change:** Add our new Instagram handle @acme_co next to the existing
> social icons.

## What happens after you submit

1. You'll see a confirmation message immediately.
2. Your request goes straight into our system.
3. We'll reply to the email you submitted with either:
   - Confirmation it's done (with a link to view the change), **or**
   - A clarifying question if we need more info.

## Urgent issues

If something on your site is **broken** (not loading, contact form not
working, etc.), use the form and pick "Same day" — or email us directly
at the address we've shared with you.

## What we can change quickly

- Text on existing pages
- Swapping images or logos
- Adding or removing items in lists (services, team members, etc.)
- Contact info, hours, social links
- Adding a new page that follows your existing design

## What takes longer

- Visual redesign of a page or section
- New features (forms, calculators, integrations)
- Anything that changes how the site is structured

We'll always tell you up front if a request is bigger than it looks.
