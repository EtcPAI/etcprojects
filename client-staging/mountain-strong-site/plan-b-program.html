The idea / 01

It is only prudent to have a Plan B.

The idea behind your Plan B is to create awareness. We live our life Plan A, but we do not always see life's challenges, even when they are right in front of us or all around us. That does not mean they are not there simply because we cannot see them.

The information in this booklet keeps all of your data in one location. It is an indispensable resource for you and your family while you live your Plan A life. More importantly, when things slip off the rails and you must implement one of your Plan B strategies, the information needed to guide you and your family will be here, thought out, in advance.

Does this sound like you?
  • You want your information in order for the people you love.
  • You'd rather decide things now, calmly, than leave your family guessing later.
  • You want everything in one place: accounts, property, policies, and wishes.
  • You want to keep living your Plan A without worrying about the what-ifs.
  • You want your plan kept private, on your own device, under your control.

Tick even one, and your Plan B is worth starting.

With a strong Plan B in place, you can forget about life's challenges and enjoy your life.

Why it matters / 02

Everything in one place, sorted out ahead of time.

When a crisis hits, the hardest part is rarely the decision. It is the scramble. Scattered passwords, a policy no one can find, an account no one knew about, papers in three different drawers. Grief and pressure make even simple things hard, and the people you love end up guessing under a deadline. Plan B does that finding now, while life is calm, so no one has to do it later.

01 / One location

All of your data, organized in one place.

Accounts, property, policies, people, and wishes. An easy way to keep all the essential documents of your life in one location, so nothing important is scattered or lost.

02 / Ready in advance

Thought out before it is needed.

When something slips off the rails, the answers your family needs are already here, decided when you were calm and clear, not guessed at under pressure.

03 / Easy to keep current

As accurate as what you put in.

The booklet is only as accurate as what you enter. Miss a detail or two and that is okay, just keep your papers with your book so everything stays together and current.

How it works / 04

Yours to fill in, at your own pace.

Plan B is a private workbook you complete in your own browser. There is no account to make and nothing to send in. You work through it whenever it suits you.

01

Request your access.

Send a short note and we will share your private link to the workbook. Clients of the practice receive it as part of their service; everyone else can request self-guided access.

02

Fill it in, section by section.

Work through the twenty sections in any order, a little at a time. Your progress is saved automatically and privately in the browser you use to complete the form, so you can stop and pick it back up whenever you like.

03

Print or download a copy.

When it is ready, print a clean copy for the shelf or download one to keep. There is also a blank version you can print and complete by hand if you prefer paper.

04

Keep it current, and keep in touch.

Store the document somewhere safe and keep your papers with it. Things change, sometimes a little and sometimes a lot, so review it with Joe when life shifts, which may only be every few years.

Good to know / 05

A few honest things, up front.

Plan B is a place to think through ideas and gather what matters. Here is exactly what it is, and what it is not.

It does not replace your legal documents.

Nothing recorded in this book overrides any legal document, including your Last Will and Testament. This planning book and everything in it is only for working through ideas.

Joe advises where he is licensed.

The book covers your investment portfolio and life insurance products. Registered with CIRO and licensed with the Life Insurance Councils of BC, AB, SK, MB, and ON, Joe can advise you on these parts of your portfolio.

He is not a lawyer or an accountant.

Much of what you plan touches legal and accounting matters. Joe is neither a lawyer nor a chartered accountant, and will tell you when an area needs one. You can use your existing lawyer or accountant for those pieces.

Review it as life changes.

The advice is current as of when the book is put together. Things change, sometimes a little, sometimes a lot, so keep in touch and review your financial affairs and estate plan regularly, even if only every few years.

Store it somewhere safe.

Because the workbook gathers so much in one place, you must keep it somewhere secure. Lost or stolen information may allow identity theft or financial theft. If your copy is ever lost or stolen, protect yourself immediately by notifying the relevant authorities, including your bank, credit card company, and insurer.
